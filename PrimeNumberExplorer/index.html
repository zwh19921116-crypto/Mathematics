What Is a Prime Number?

A prime number is a whole number greater than 1 that has exactly 2 factors: 1 and itself.

  • Prime examples: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11
  • Not prime: 1 (only one factor), 4 (1, 2, 4), 9 (1, 3, 9)

Quick rule: If a number can be divided by another whole number (besides 1 and itself), it is not prime.
